# What are AI and AGI?

Here is a clear breakdown of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Artificial General Intelligence (AGI), and the key differences between them.

## Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Artificial Intelligence is a broad field of computer science focused on creating systems capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence.

Today's AI is often called **Narrow AI** (or Weak AI) because it is trained to do a specific set of tasks. It is excellent at finding patterns in data and making predictions within its trained domain, but it cannot operate outside of it.

**Examples of current AI:**

* Voice assistants (like Siri or Google Assistant) understanding your speech.
* Algorithms recommending what video to watch next.
* Self-driving cars navigating roads.
* Large Language Models (like GPT or Gemini) generating text or translating languages.

## Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)

Artificial General Intelligence is a **theoretical** future form of AI. An AGI system would possess the ability to understand, learn, and apply its intelligence to solve *any* problem, matching or exceeding human cognitive abilities across the board.

While current AI is highly specialized, an AGI would be highly adaptable. It wouldn't just be good at one specific task; it could teach itself to write a complex web application, compose a symphony, and design a bridge, all without needing to be fundamentally rebuilt or reprogrammed by humans for each new domain.

## The Main Difference: Specialization vs. Versatility

* **AI (What we have now):** Can beat a grandmaster at chess, but doesn't know what a chessboard is made of or how to make a cup of coffee. It is **specialized**.
* **AGI (What researchers are aiming for):** Could learn to play chess, then go to the kitchen, figure out how the coffee machine works, and make a cup of coffee. It is **versatile**.
